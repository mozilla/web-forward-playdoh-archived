Web FWD FAQ

General

  • How does this work?
    In a nutshell: Apply with your project (which fits into the predefined interest areas and will be built with the traits that are important for the open Web), get accepted, come to San Francisco for our 4 week camps, build/learn/mingle, launch your product, decide how to take it forward. More details can be found here.
  • Will this happen in cities other than San Francisco (and other countries than the US)?
    Oh yes. We just felt it's best to do our first iteration(s) close to home.
  • Why do you do this?
    We mainly have three reasons to do this (and not to forget to mention-we just love working with people such as yourself):
    • Spur innovation & develop solutions in the predefined problem spaces/areas
    • Support you to build these solutions with the traits that are important for the open Web
    • Bolster Mozilla's effectiveness in the world through this program
  • Who's behind this program?
    Mozilla (we're probably best known as the maker of the Firefox web browser). More specifically Mozilla Labs - the part of the Mozilla organization where we tinker with and explore the fringes of what's possible today on the Web.
  • What will I eat and where will I sleep during the 4 week program?
    We give an allowance of $2,000 per team member for the 4 weeks of the program (up to $6,000 total per team). This is enough money to find a nice apartment and eat plenty of food (not only ramen). We also have plenty of free coffee.
  • How much control do I give up for joining your program?
    Nothing. Nada. Nichts. Did we say "we are different"? We want you to build something which has the potential to change the world. And we're sure you remember us when you strike that pot of gold at the end of the rainbow. Because that's what we would do for you. But other than that - nothing, really. We want you to bring your idea into the Mozilla community, we want to learn from you (at least as much as you learn from us), we want you to write Open Source code and we want you to build an awesome product. That's all.
  • Can we turn our idea into a business?
    Sure. And we're happy to help as much as we can. In particular Pascal is interested in developing new business models for Open Source companies and popularizing them - as he believes that there is a lot of innovation in this area.
  • This sounds (insert your term of excitement here)! What's the catch?
    None. Seriously. And if you still don't believe us - email us at 'webfwd at mozilla.com', ping us on IRC or if you're in the area just swing by. We're also attending a bunch of events where you can meet with us.

Application Process

  • Do I need a prototype?
    Yes. And no. You don't need a full fledged prototype. But you need more than just an idea. You need code, mockups, something tangible. Most of all - you actually need to be able to pull this off and be able to build your product/service.
  • What are you looking for in a founding team?
    Mainly the ability to deliver what you set out to do. This often means a great combination of core skills. If your team is missing key skills it's not a deal-breaker but it surely will be much harder.
  • Do you only take teams?
    Not necessarily. But it is certainly easier to build a product if you're not a lone soldier but come as a group of people with strong core skills.
  • Can I only apply if I'm a US citizen/live in the US?
    No. We love to have people from diverse cultural backgrounds in the program - having said that: For the first iteration(s) you must be able to legally work in the US for the duration of the program.
  • Am I too young or too old for your program?
    As long as you fulfill the legal requirement (i.e. you are at least 18 years old) we are golden.
  • Can we do it without moving to where you are?
    No. Sorry to be firm on this one - but the whole program is designed around the fact that you are here, working next to us. And believe us - it's much more fun to do this in person anyway.
  • Do I need to work on this full-time?
    We understand that you might not have worked on your project full-time until now. And that's okay. But when you come here, it's time for the rubber to hit the proverbial road - and for you to do this all-in and full-time. It'll be fun. And rewarding. And a great ride. So don't worry.
  • Can I apply with a product/service even if it doesn't fit into one of those areas?
    Yes. But be aware of the fact that we primarily look for solutions which fit into the areas we identified as our interest foci.
  • What is the selection process?
    We have a selection committee who will select the participating teams based on fit to our interest areas, overall compliance with the traits we care about and (obviously) potential of idea and team. We might invite a few of you to join us for follow-up calls/video conferences or in-person meetings if we have questions.
  • Why didn't you choose me?
    Don't fret. Us not choosing you is by no means a statement about your skills or your idea. You just might not be a good fit for the program at this moment in time.
  • What's the deadline for my application?
    There is no deadline. We have a rolling submission process and accept projects at pretty much any time.
  • Do I need a business plan (and what is this anyway)?
    No, we don't require a business plan. And if you don't know what this is - no worries. We'll figure out together.
  • We are already incorporated (a company) - can we still apply?
    Yes, that's not a deal-breaker. As long as you're willing to comply with our requirements.

Camp

  • What happens during the 4 week camp?
    First and foremost - you will build your product. In the process of doing so you will meet with other Mozillians, exchange ideas, get critiqued, learn and present. About two or three nights a week we'll organize sessions with our mentors. We'll organize a couple of cool events around the program which give you even more chance to mingle and network. We will make sure you'll have a great time.
  • What happens after the 4 week camp?
    At the end of the 4 week camp you will present your product to the wider Mozilla community. You will make a decision on how to take this forward (with which we will help you). After that it's a highly individual process - as there are so many variables in this game. Rest assured - we're here to help.
  • Who are your mentors & experts?
    Here is a complete list. Generally speaking they are all amazing experts in their respective fields. And a lot of fun to hang out with.
  • What is a typical mentoring session?
    Typically a mentor spends some time with all teams in a (usually highly interactive) presentation, then goes on to sit down with individual teams to answer questions, provide insights, give direction, etc. Often mentors hang around for a drink or two in the evening.
  • What else do you provide? Office space, infrastructure, ramen?
    A whole bunch of cool stuff - you get a desk in our of our beautiful, brimming with great energy MozSpaces; we help you with servers, software, UI, UX, design... the list goes on and on. Basically all the stuff you need to get your project off the ground (and don't get us wrong - it's not that we're doing all this for you - sorry lazy bums... But we are here to help!).
  • What about networking (not the cable-type but the meat-space type)
    Plenty of it. The power of networking can't be underestimated. More often then not it's the people you know who will make or break a particular idea. So we make sure you meet lots of the right people. And if networking is something which doesn't come naturally for you - we'll help.

Other

  • What do you exactly mean when you say my technology must be Open Source?
    Simple: All the code you write during the program (and as a prerequisite the code you base your work on during the program) needs to be released under a tri-license (MPL, GPL and LGPL) or similarly open license. This is exactly the same way we release our software including Firefox.
  • What if my project turns out to be a dud?
    No worries. Such is life (sometimes). The world will not end.
  • Are you looking for mentors and other people & organizations which can help?
    Yes. Please send us an email at 'webfwd at mozilla.com'.
  • Are you hiring?
    In fact we are. Check out Mozilla's open job listings here.
